## Answer The punishment for robbery varies depending on the jurisdiction, the severity of the crime, and the specific laws in place. In general, robbery is considered a serious crime and is punishable by imprisonment. The severity of the punishment can range from a few years to life imprisonment, depending on the circumstances of the crime. In the United States, for example, robbery is typically classified as a felony, and the punishment can range from 5 to 20 years in prison, depending on the state and the specifics of the crime. In some cases, the punishment can be even more severe, such as in cases where the robbery involves a weapon or results in serious bodily harm to the victim. In addition to imprisonment, those convicted of robbery may also face fines, restitution to the victim, and probation or parole.
